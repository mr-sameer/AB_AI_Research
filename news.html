What Makes The Cut

Not every headline — only what moves the thesis

This isn't a news feed of everything a company does. Every alert has to pass a materiality test: does it actually change the numbers, the risk, or the story an investor should be tracking?

✓ Makes the cut

  • Earnings beats/misses and guidance changes
  • Major deal wins, losses, or contract terminations
  • CEO/CFO or key leadership changes
  • Order backlog, capex, or utilization data shifts
  • Export controls, tariffs, and policy changes affecting the sector
  • M&A activity — acquisitions, divestitures, stake sales
  • Wage hikes, attrition trends, or currency moves hitting margins
  • Visible share shifts between competitors

✕ Filtered out

  • Generic product-launch PR with no revenue/margin angle
  • Day-to-day price moves with no underlying cause
  • Award, recognition, or "vision statement" press releases
  • Rehashed wire copy with nothing new versus what's priced in
  • Sustainability/CSR announcements with no financial link
  • Speculation with no source behind it
