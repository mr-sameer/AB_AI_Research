How I Work

The same five checks, every single report

This is the actual sequence — not a manifesto. It's here so you can judge whether I followed my own process, not just take my word for the conclusion.

  • 01
    Filter the noise

    Start from the filing and the transcript, not the headline. Most "news" is a restatement of something already priced in.

  • 02
    Read past the press release

    Compare what management says against what the numbers, hiring data, and channel checks actually show.

  • 03
    Map the numbers to the narrative

    Margins, order books, and capex only mean something once they're tied back to the story the market is telling itself.

  • 04
    Flag the risk nobody's pricing in

    Every report names the one thing that could break the thesis — not buried in a footnote, but up front.

  • 05
    Take a position, publicly

    A view with no stance is just a data dump. I say what I think, and I revisit it when I'm wrong.
